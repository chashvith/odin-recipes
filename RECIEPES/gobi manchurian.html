Gobi manchurian

description:

Gobi Manchurian is an Indo-Chinese appetizer and this version will blow your mind! It features ridiculously crispy and crunchy fried cauliflower coated in a sweet, tangy, umami-rich chili sauce with lots of aromatics. A MUST-make for your next party or get-together!

Ingredients

  • For the Gobi (Cauliflower):
    • Cauliflower – Cut into bite-sized florets
    • Corn Flour – For the batter and for crispiness
    • All-purpose Flour (Maida) – To help bind the batter
    • Rice Flour – Adds crispiness
    • Ginger-garlic paste – For flavor
    • Salt – To taste
    • Black Pepper – For seasoning
    • Water – To make the batter
    • Oil – For deep frying
  • For the Manchurian Sauce:
    • Oil – To sauté the sauce ingredients
    • Garlic – Minced or finely chopped
    • Ginger – Finely chopped or grated
    • Onions – Chopped
    • Bell Peppers – Chopped (optional, but adds crunch and flavor)
    • Green Chilies – Slit or chopped, for heat
    • Soy Sauce – For the umami flavor
    • Chili Sauce – Adds spice and tang
    • Tomato Ketchup – For sweetness and a tangy flavor
    • Vinegar – For tanginess
    • Sugar – A pinch (optional, to balance flavors)
    • Cornstarch – Mixed with water to thicken the sauce
    • Spring Onions – Chopped (for garnish)
    • Salt – To taste
  • Optional Garnish:
    • Sesame Seeds – For crunch
    • Coriander Leaves – For freshness

steps to make

  1. Wash and cut the cauliflower into bite-sized florets.
  2. Blanch the cauliflower in boiling water for 2-3 minutes, then drain and set aside.
  3. In a bowl, mix corn flour, all-purpose flour, rice flour, ginger-garlic paste, salt, and pepper to make a thick batter.
  4. Dip the cauliflower florets into the batter and coat them evenly.
  5. Heat oil in a pan and fry the battered cauliflower until golden and crispy. Drain on paper towels.
  6. In another pan, heat oil and sauté minced garlic, ginger, and green chilies for 1-2 minutes.
  7. Add chopped onions and bell peppers, and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  8. Mix in soy sauce, chili sauce, and ketchup, then cook for another minute.
  9. Add vinegar and a pinch of sugar (optional), and stir to combine.
  10. Make a cornstarch slurry with water, add it to the sauce, and cook until the sauce thickens.
  11. Add the fried cauliflower florets to the sauce and toss gently to coat.
  12. Garnish with spring onions, sesame seeds, and coriander leaves. Serve hot.
